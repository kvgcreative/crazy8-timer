Crazy Eights

How to use the timer and run a team session

What is it?

Crazy 8s is a rapid sketching exercise from Google Ventures' Design Sprint. Each person folds a sheet of paper into 8 panels and has one minute to sketch a different idea in each one. The time pressure forces quantity over quality — and that's the whole point.

The goal isn't polished drawings. It's to move past your first obvious idea and explore a wider range of possibilities before committing to a direction.

When each minute ends, a "Move to next sketch!" overlay appears with an audio alert and auto-closes after 5 seconds. At the end of the full session, a "Pencils down!" screen plays a victory chime.

Facilitating a team session

Before you start

  1. Give everyone a sheet of paper and have them fold it in half three times to create 8 equal panels.
  2. Define the challenge clearly — everyone should be sketching toward the same problem.
  3. Remind the team these are quick sketches, not wireframes. Stick figures and rough boxes are perfect.
  4. Open this timer on a shared screen, or have each person run it on their own device.

During the session

  1. Press Start — everyone sketches simultaneously, one idea per panel.
  2. When the alert sounds, everyone moves to the next panel immediately.
  3. Discourage talking during the exercise — stay heads-down until "Pencils down!" plays.
  4. Encourage participants to number each panel as they go to keep track.

After the session

  1. Each person picks their single strongest idea and puts a star on it.
  2. Go around the room — each person presents their starred sketch in 60 seconds or less.
  3. Use dot voting or a heat map to identify which ideas the team finds most promising.
  4. Take the top 1–3 concepts forward into more detailed prototyping or decision-making.
Tips for great sessions
Silence is goldenNo talking during sketching — conversation kills flow and slows everyone down.
Quantity over quality8 rough ideas beats 2 polished ones. Ugly sketches are encouraged.
Label everythingA quick title or caption makes sketches far easier to present afterward.
Vary the timeTry 2-min slots for complex problems, 30-sec for pure ideation speed runs.
Remote teamsUse a shared Miro or FigJam board — upload phone photos right after.
Warm up firstA 5-minute "draw anything" exercise reduces self-consciousness before you begin.
